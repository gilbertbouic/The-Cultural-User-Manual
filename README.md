### United States

**Country covered:** United States of America

The United States is a large, diverse country with a culture that prioritizes individualism, directness, and a friendly, informal communication style. While regional differences are significant, several core values and behaviors are broadly shared.

**Distinguishing features:**
- **Informality:** First names are used almost immediately in most situations, including with superiors.
- **Direct Communication:** Americans tend to be direct and explicit in their communication. They generally say what they mean.
- **Individualism:** High value is placed on personal achievement, independence, and self-reliance.
- **Customer Service Culture:** A strong emphasis is placed on customer satisfaction, often with a "customer is always right" attitude.
- **Tipping:** Tipping is expected and considered mandatory in many service situations (restaurants, taxis, etc.) and is not included in the bill.
- **Enthusiasm:** Displaying enthusiasm and a positive, "can-do" attitude is often valued in social and professional settings.
- **Punctuality:** Being on time for appointments and meetings is expected.

### Scandinavia

**Countries covered:** Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Iceland

Scandinavia represents the Nordic cultural heartland, characterized by a strong emphasis on egalitarianism, social welfare, and a balanced approach to life. The Swedish concept of "lagom" (not too much, not too little) pervades regional attitudes toward work, consumption, and social interaction.

**Distinguishing features:**
- **Social equality:** Flat hierarchies in workplaces; first-name basis is common even with bosses
- **Personal space:** High value placed on privacy and physical distance in public settings
- **Fika culture:** Regular coffee breaks are important social rituals, especially in Sweden
- **Environmental consciousness:** Strong commitment to sustainability and nature access (allemansrÃ¤ttenâ€”right to roam)
- **Seasonal awareness:** Life rhythms adapt significantly to extreme seasonal light variations
- **Reserved communication:** Silence is comfortable; small talk is less prevalent than in other cultures

---

### Finland

**Country covered:** Finland

Finland stands apart from its Nordic neighbors with a unique language (non-Germanic), distinctive traditions, and cultural values. While sharing some traits with Scandinavia, Finnish culture has its own character shaped by geography and history.

**Distinguishing features:**
- **Sisu:** A cultural concept meaning determination, resilience, and inner strength in adversity
- **Sauna culture:** Central to Finnish social life; a space for relaxation, cleansing, and even business discussions
- **Comfort with silence:** Finns are famously comfortable with pauses in conversation; silence is not awkward
- **Direct communication:** Honesty and straightforwardness are valued; less indirect than other Nordic nations
- **Educational excellence:** World-renowned education system with emphasis on equality and well-being
- **Nature connection:** Deep relationship with forests, lakes, and outdoor activities year-round

---

### United Kingdom

**Countries covered:** England, Scotland, Wales, Northern Ireland

The United Kingdom represents a distinct cultural sphere characterized by reserve, indirect communication, and a rich tapestry of regional identities. Despite internal diversity, shared traits define the broader British cultural experience.

**Distinguishing features:**
- **Queuing:** Sacred social ritual; never skip or disrespect the queue
- **Indirect communication:** Understatement, irony, and saying the opposite of what is meant are common
- **Essential phrases:** "Please," "thank you," and "sorry" are used constantly and expected
- **Pub culture:** The local pub serves as a community hub; "rounds" system for buying drinks
- **Class awareness:** Subtle but present social distinctions; accent and vocabulary carry social signals
- **Self-deprecating humor:** Making fun of oneself is valued; boasting is frowned upon
- **Tea culture:** Tea breaks are important social and workplace rituals

### Balkans

**Countries covered:** Serbia, Croatia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Montenegro, North Macedonia, Albania, Kosovo, Slovenia

The Balkans represent one of the world's most culturally diverse regions. Rather than selecting a single national archetype, this grouping acknowledges the rich tapestry of traditions, languages, and histories that characterize Southeastern regions.

### Greece

**Country covered:** Greece

Greece occupies a unique cultural position bridging Southern regions and the Eastern Mediterranean. Its ancient heritage, Orthodox Christian traditions, and Mediterranean lifestyle create a distinctive cultural environment.

**Distinguishing features:**
- **Philoxenia (hospitality):** Deep cultural tradition of welcoming strangers; generosity to guests is a point of honor
- **Mediterranean pace:** Relaxed approach to time; social relationships take priority over schedules
- **Family primacy:** Extended family ties are central; family businesses and gatherings are common
- **Orthodox traditions:** Religious calendar influences social life; name days celebrated more than birthdays
- **Animated conversation:** Expressive communication with gestures; comfortable with debate and disagreement
- **Coffee culture:** Extended cafÃ© visits are social institutions; Greek coffee rituals are important
- **Siesta tradition:** Afternoon rest period (though declining in urban areas)
- **Pride in heritage:** Strong national identity connected to ancient Greek civilization

**Representatives:** Italy, France  
**Countries covered:** Italy, France, Spain, Portugal

The Mediterranean cultural zone shares characteristics shaped by climate, geography, and historical connections. While each nation maintains distinct identity, common threads unite the region.

- **Formal/informal distinction:** Vous/tu (French), Lei/tu (Italian) distinctions in address are important
- **Work-life balance:** Quality of life prioritized; lunch breaks are substantial; August vacations are sacred
- **Expressive communication:** More animated gestures and emotional expression than Northern regions
- **Aesthetic appreciation:** Art, fashion, design, and beauty are valued in daily life
- **Regional pride:** Strong local and regional identities; cuisine and traditions vary significantly by area
- **Social dining:** Never rush through meals; eating alone is less common; meals are communal events
